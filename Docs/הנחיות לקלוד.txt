Key MBTI Components (The 4 Dichotomies)The 16 types are generated from a combination of preferences across four categories: Extraversion (E) - Introversion (I): How you direct your energy. Extraverts focus on the outer world (people/activities), while Introverts focus on their inner world (thoughts/ideas).Sensing (S) - Intuition (N): How you take in information. Sensors focus on facts, details, and concrete realities, while Intuitives focus on possibilities, patterns, and abstract ideas.Thinking (T) - Feeling (F): How you make decisions. Thinkers make decisions based on logic, objective evidence, and principles, while Feelers consider emotions, values, and the impact on others.Judging (J) - Perceiving (P): How you approach the outer world. Judgers prefer structure, plans, and closure, while Perceivers prefer flexibility, spontaneity, and keeping options open.
